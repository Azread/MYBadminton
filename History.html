History

The predecessor of badminton was cricket (but now badminton and cricket has evolved into two different ball sports), ie use a wooden board to hit a furry ball (similar to a feather dodge), and let it dodge the game. With almost history 2000 years, it can be seen in ancient Europe, China and Japan. Among them, a type of badminton board comes from ancient Greece, and it developed to convert solid wood rackets into wooden frames, and tie a tight sheepskin in the middle, which is easier to beaten for its elasticity. The game spread east to India ancient and as far as Siam, China and Japan. However, the purpose the game is to keep the ball in the air as much as possible without landing, which is very different from the spirit of modern badminton.

In the middle of the 19th century, modern badminton appeared in Pune, west India, which was called by the place name "Poona" at that time. Because The local British loved this new sport, they brought it back to British mainland. In 1873, a public performance was held at Badminton Manor in Gloucestershire, England, which is interesting the attention of many people and gradually spread. Then, the people call the sport by the name of the manor of the show, "Badminton". However, in Chinese-speaking areas, this name is not popularized, and it is called "Badminton" according to the equipment.

Early British badminton still used the unwritten rules of India, but it is often controversial for not being strict enough. In 1887, "Bath Badminton Club" researched and improved, and solve the first written badminton rules. In 1893, The English Badminton Association was set up to review and unifying the rules of the game of badminton. In 1898, Open Guilford, considered the first official open competition badminton, has been successfully held in Guildford, Surrey, England, and leading to the longest running open badminton, the Open All England, held on April 4.

In 1934, the first worldwide badminton organization, The International Badminton Federation (IBF), was established in the United Kingdom. In 1981, it merged with the Badminton Federation World (WBF), which was established in 1978, and its name is still International Badminton Federation. On September 24, 2006, The International Badminton Federation has changed its name to Badminton World Federation (BWF). Now there are 159 countries or member territory.
